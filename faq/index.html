Scrum Master Interview FAQs

Practical, scenario-based questions and answers to help you prepare effectively.

Interviews for Scrum Master roles often go beyond theory—they test your ability to handle real-world challenges in Agile environments. Below are some of the most common scenario-based questions (like those asked in companies such as Wipro), along with practical answers to help you stand out.

1. How do you ensure your team remains motivated during long and complex projects?

I focus on celebrating small wins, maintaining transparency, and ensuring work is meaningful. Regular check-ins, feedback sessions, and giving autonomy help sustain motivation throughout long projects.

2. What would you do if your team consistently misses sprint commitments?

I would analyze patterns, ensure stories are well-refined and right-sized, and encourage committing based on velocity rather than optimism. The goal is to improve predictability rather than chase stretch goals.

3. How do you handle situations where the Product Owner is unavailable for backlog refinement?

I ensure the team has a prioritized backlog prepared in advance, and if the PO is unavailable, I collaborate with stakeholders or use proxy inputs to avoid blocking refinement sessions.

4. How do you deal with team members who resist Agile practices?

I listen to their concerns, provide real examples of Agile benefits, and gradually introduce practices that address their pain points. Resistance often reduces once they see results.

5. What approach do you take when the team delivers quality work but the stakeholders are still unhappy?

I bridge the gap by improving communication, involving stakeholders earlier, and ensuring clear definition of “done” and acceptance criteria. Often, expectations alignment solves the disconnect.

6. How would you coach a team that treats daily stand-ups like status update meetings?

I would coach them to focus on collaboration by emphasizing: what was done, what will be done, and what blockers exist. I’d redirect the mindset from reporting to problem-solving.

7. How do you balance delivery pressure from management and protecting the team’s sustainable pace?

I advocate for the team’s capacity with management, use velocity trends as evidence, and reinforce the value of sustainable pace to prevent burnout and quality issues.

8. What steps do you take to improve collaboration between developers and testers in the same team?

I encourage pairing, cross-functional refinement sessions, and joint ownership of quality. Shared goals and early testing practices (shift-left) improve collaboration.

9. How do you ensure continuous improvement when the team is unwilling to discuss failures in retrospectives?

I create a safe, blame-free environment, start with anonymous inputs, and highlight learning opportunities rather than failures. This builds trust and openness gradually.

10. How do you deal with external dependencies that repeatedly block your sprint progress?

I make dependencies visible on the board, escalate recurring blockers, and work with stakeholders to resolve them. Where possible, we aim to reduce dependencies through cross-skilling or architecture changes.

11. What metrics do you track as a Scrum Master, and how do you use them to improve team performance?

I track velocity, sprint predictability, cycle time, and defect trends. I use these not for control, but for insights—helping the team reflect, forecast better, and identify bottlenecks.

12. How do you handle a situation where a senior developer dominates sprint planning discussions and overshadows others?

I would set ground rules for equal participation, encourage quieter voices, and where needed, have a one-on-one with the senior developer to align on collaborative behavior.
